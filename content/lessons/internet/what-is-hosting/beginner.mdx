# What is Hosting?

Imagine you've written a beautiful letter and want to share it with the world. You can't just leave it on your desk and expect everyone to read it — you need to put it somewhere people can access it, like a public bulletin board.

**Web hosting** is like renting space on that bulletin board for your website. It's a service that stores your website files on a powerful computer (called a server) that's connected to the Internet 24/7.

## The Apartment Analogy

Think of web hosting like renting an apartment:

- **Your website files** are your furniture and belongings
- **The hosting server** is the apartment building
- **The hosting company** is your landlord
- **Your domain name** is your street address

Just like you pay rent to live in an apartment, you pay a hosting company to keep your website online and accessible to visitors around the world.

## How Does It Work?

When someone types your website address (like `www.mysite.com`) into their browser:

1. **The browser asks**: "Where does this website live?"
2. **DNS responds**: "It lives at this server address: 192.168.1.1"
3. **The browser visits**: The server at that address
4. **The server sends**: Your website files back to the browser
5. **The browser displays**: Your beautiful website!

All of this happens in milliseconds — faster than you can blink!

<InfoBox type="tip" title="Why Can't I Host From My Computer?">
Technically, you could! But your home computer would need to be on 24/7, have a fast and stable internet connection, and handle potentially thousands of visitors. Hosting companies have specialized equipment and infrastructure designed exactly for this purpose.
</InfoBox>

## What You Need to Know

As a beginner, here are the key takeaways:

<KeyConcept title="Hosting = Storage + Availability">
Web hosting stores your website files and makes them available to anyone on the Internet, anytime.
</KeyConcept>

<KeyConcept title="You Need Both Domain + Hosting">
A domain name is your address, hosting is where you actually live. You need both for a working website!
</KeyConcept>

<KeyConcept title="Start Simple">
For most beginners, shared hosting is perfect. It's affordable, easy to set up, and the hosting company handles all the technical stuff.
</KeyConcept>
